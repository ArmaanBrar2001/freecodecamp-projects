Captain Vikram Batra(SHER SHAH)


Hero of the Kargil War(1999)


Capt. Vikram Batra is holding a rifle in his hands

The timeline of Capt. Vikram Batra's life is as follows:



  • Early Life:

    • 1974 - Born in Himachal Pradesh, India
    • Father - Girdhari Lal Batra(Government School Principal)
    • Mother - Kamal Kanta Batra(School Teacher)
    • Siblings - Elder of twin sons and had two sisters


  • Education:

    • Middle Standard - D.A.V Public School, Palampur
    • Senior Secondary - Central School, Palampur
    • Bachelor's Degree - D.A.V College, Chandigarh
    • Bachelor's Degree - D.A.V College, Chandigarh(B.Sc Medical Sciences)
    • Master's Degree - Punjab University, Chandigarh(M.A English)
    • 1995 - Selected for Merchant Navy
    • 1996 - Passed CDS(Combined Defence Services) exam


  • Military Career:

    • June 1996 - Joined Indian Military Academy(IMA) in the Manekshaw Battalion
    • December 1997 - Commissioned as a lieutenant into the 13th battalion, Jammu and Kashmir Rifles (13 JAK RIF)
    • September 1998 - Completed Young Officer's Course at infantry school in Mhow, Madhya Pradesh where he was awarded Alpha Grading
    • March 1999 - Completed Commando Course at Belgaum, Karnataka and there he was awarded with Instructor's Grade


  • Kargil War and Martyrdom:

    • 6 June 1999 - 13 JAK RIF reached Dras under the command of 56 Mountain Brigade and were reserves to 2nd battalion
    • 20 Jun 1999 - Captured Point 5140 along with Lt. Sanjeev Singh Jamwal under the command of Lt. Col. Yogesh Kumar Joshi
    • 27 June 1999 - Batra was promoted to the rank of Captain
    • 30 June 1999 - 13 JAK RIF moved to Mushkoh Valley to capture Point 4875
    • 7 July 1999 - While evacuating one of the injured soldier, Capt. Vikram Batra was shot in the chest by an enemy sniper from a very close range and a split-second later, by a splinter from an RPG which hit him in the head. Batra collapsed next to the injured soldier, succumbing to his fatal wounds


  • 15 August 1999- Vikram Batra was awarded Param Vir Chakra, the highest award in military ranking posthumously. His father G.L. Batra recieved the award
